About Me

I earned my Bachelors in Math and Physics at Cal Poly Pomona and am currently the lead instructor at the Mathnasium of Woodland Hills. I worked as a math tutor at an afterschool program at Crenshaw HS and have a high success rate of improving students' performance. I work with kids ranging from 1st grade up to the 12th grade, covering Basic Math up to Calculus I. My instruction style is direct and conceptual, where I focus on building up the thought process and motivations leading up to the topic of discussion. My experience in math includes applying it to Physics, Machine Learning, and Robotics. I am currently applying to graduate programs for Math with the goal of teaching at the college level.

Is Private Tutoring Right for You?

Flexibility

Falling behind in class requires proper guidance to get back on track. Although possible, attempting to teach yourself the material by reading the textbook is unlikely to work as textbooks generally serve as a reference. Scouring resources online can help but variations such as in notation and ordering of topics covered can lead to confusion. . Learning math at this stage is rote as repetition is necessary to associate symbols with the intuitive notion of counting. The concepts of adding, substracting, multiplication, and division are built on this notion of counting. For this age group, tutoring centers are a good choice as their curriculums are systemic. Students are put into group sessions where the instructors undivided attention isn't needed.

Fill in the Gaps

Math builds upwards. Any uncertainty in previous material will surface. We have to revisit the material to fill in the gaps and solidify any uncertainties.
